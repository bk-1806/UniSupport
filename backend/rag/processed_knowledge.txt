He envisaged universities as inclusive institutions

of higher learning that transcend linguistic, racial, and other barriers. GITAM is deeply committed to imbibing Mahatma Gandhi’s values and philosophy, promoting inclusivity and diversity in education, and integrating ethical and social responsibility into all aspects of its academic and extracurricular activities.

10. Attendance Policy Students are expected to maintain 100% attendance on all registered courses. However, a minimum overall attendance of 75% across all courses in a semester is mandatory. Only students who meet this minimum requirement shall be permitted to appear for Sessional-I and Sessional-II examinations. Further details regarding attendance requirements are provided in the Evaluation Policy.

11. Leave Policy Students are expected to maintain 100% attendance in all courses. However, students with 75% or more attendance will be permitted to appear for the sessional examinations.

The University encourages participation in sports and extracurricular activities. While such involvement is supported, students must ensure that their attendance does not fall below 75%.

Attendance shortage may be considered for exemption only in the following exceptional cases: • Representing the University, State, or Country in approved events. • Medical emergencies, supported by valid documentation.

11.1 Representing the University, State or Country The Vice-Chancellor, on the recommendation of the HOI of the School and remarks from the Director, Student life, GITAM may condone the shortage of attendance of the students on the grounds of participation in co-curricular and extracurricular activities representing the University, State or Country.

11.2 Medical Emergency The student can apply for medical leave during the programme of study in the format given in Annexure. The process to be followed in Medical Emergency is given below • The student needs to produce documents from a registered medical practitioner to the Mentor. • Mentor will acknowledge after verifying the documents by email to the student and the guardian. • The Mentor will submit the documents to the HoI; The HoI or his representative will discuss the leave request with the

medical officer of the University. • The medical officer will approve the number of days the student can avail themselves of medical leave based on the documents submitted by the student. • The student's status changes from "Active" to "Medical Leave of absence" during medical leave. For the course duration, the student may have a maximum of two years of medical leave. The student can avail of this leave at a stretch or in multiple phases. • However, this medical leave could be converted into an

Academic Break. The student is not allowed to utilize the physical resources and infrastructure of GITAM during the academic break.

Before applying for rejoining after an academic break or medical leave, the student must produce the medical fitness certificate from the medical officer of the University. Students should reinitiate the rejoining process at least 30 days before the due date of sanctioned leave. The student under medical leave of absence should communicate with the Mentor for rejoining or extension of leave. The student can cancel their approved period of absence and apply for rejoining before the commencement of

a semester. The unauthorized absence of a student shall be treated as a withdrawal from the programme.

11.3 Parental leave of absence Students who wish to take a break during their study period due to parental reasons such as pregnancy, maternity or paternity to take care of their child can apply for parental leave of absence. On the recommendation of the HOI of the School and remarks from the medical officer, the Vice-Chancellor can permit the student to write the sessional examinations. The process to be followed is given below: • The student has to submit the documents to the mentor/AMC. • The

mentor will acknowledge and forward the same to the HoD/HoI, who will convey the University's decision. • The student's status changes from "Active" to "Parental Leave of absence". • For the course duration, the student may avail a maximum of two years as parental leave. The leave can be availed at a stretch or in multiple phases. • The student is not allowed to utilize the physical resources and infrastructure of GITAM during the leave period. While applying for parental leave, the student should

mention the approximate date of rejoining. • The unauthorized absence of the student shall be treated as a withdrawal from the programme. • However, this parental leave of absence could be converted into an Academic Break.

13. Conduct and Discipline 13.1 Classroom conduct The students should attend to the academic work as per the schedule on all working days following the guidelines specified by the Director, Student Life. The students are expected to work with their classmates and Faculty members to achieve optimal learning outcomes. Students are expected to perform the assigned work within the due dates mentioned by the faculty members and must not leave the class without permission. Students should follow the guidelines

specified by the faculty member at the beginning of the course and maintain low noise levels in the class. Students should maintain a conducive environment in the classroom for the effective teaching & learning process. Classrooms or laboratory rooms should not be used for consuming food or for celebrations.

13.2 Academic Honesty Students are expected to perform the academic work in a fair manner, thereby maintaining the University's academic reputation. Any academic misconduct, such as plagiarism, malpractice in the examination, etc., will lead to remedial action suggested by the Suspected Malpractices Committee.

13.3 Violation of Academic Policy It includes the following acts, but is not limited to: i. Violating the rules and regulations of the academic programme. ii. Copying material from sources like articles, websites, etc., without proper citation and incorporating the content into the assignment and reports. iii. Any cheating in the examination, such as • Possessing unauthorized material • Taking somebody's examination. • Having somebody else take the examination, etc.
